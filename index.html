About Me

In the Beginning

I can remember the curiosity overriding my ability to sleep, the excitement I felt. I remember smelling that new plastic smell, the aroma of the protective clear coat used to protect the circuit boards of electronics before it had a chance to completely dry. It was my birthday and I just turned seven. It was 1985 and I just opened the only present I got. It was my first computer, The Commodore 64. The 64 was the amount of memory it had. 64 kilobytes or 65,536 bytes of RAM. That was 32 years ago. Ever since that day I have been coding. I started with C64 BASIC. I still remember the 10 PRINT CLS command, which cleared the screen.

Creative Problem Solving Thru Code

I would spend the majority of my life learning everything I could about how these amazing machines, these works of engineering genius. Each microchip was like a canvas, a thin silicon wafer, that the artist would use to etch their masterpieces on. I too wanted to be an artist, using code to control these ultimate machines.

Creating creative code to solve complex problems is what I love to do.
